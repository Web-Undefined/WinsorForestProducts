Species

Eastern White Pine

Eastern White Pine is our most commonly sawn product. It has a light brown (sometimes reddish) heartwood and nearly white sapwood that tends to darken with age. It is straight grained and easy to work. It is a very soft wood and may required extra care to avoid dents.

Common Uses: Construction Framing, Siding, Flooring, Fencing

Rot Resistance: Moderate decay resistance

Red Oak

Red Oak is our most commonly sawn hardwood product. Red Oak flooring is one of the most popular used in America. The heartwood is a light to medium red/brown. Grain is straight, with a coarse, uneven texture. It is highly resistant to dents and deep scratches

Common Uses: Trailer Decking, Furniture, Flooring, Fencing

Rot Resistance: Poor decay resistance

Red Maple

Red Maple is our second most commonly sawn hardwood product. The sapwood color ranges from almost white, to a light golden or reddish brown, whereas the heartwood is a darker reddish brown. Maple can sometimes be found with curly grain patterns or may be spalted adding to its character.

Common Uses: Furniture, Flooring, Wood Turning, Pallet Making

Rot Resistance: Poor decay resistance

Slabs

Wood slabs are able to be milled up to 40-inches wide. Typically slabs range in thickness from 1.5-inch - 4-inches but thicker or thinner slabs can be cut. Slabs can be cut with or without the live edge or a combination of both to meet your needs.

Firewood Slab Bundles

Bundles of slab and edge cuts are available for pickup. Each bundle is approximately 4' wide x 4' tall x 12' long.

Rounds

Wood rounds are slices of wood that are cut perpendicular to the wood grain. We are able to cut wood rounds up to 40-inch diameter and up to 10-inches thick. Wood rounds are generally unstable so expect checks (cracks) to form in the wood as it dries.

Sawdust

Please inquire about sawdust availability. Sawdust is generally comprised of eastern white pine, red oak, and red maple.
